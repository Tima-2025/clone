Concerto Jubileo 40

The Content Concerto Jubileo 40 is an organ built in celebration of the 40th anniversary of Content Organs in 2020. It incorporates a high-quality Kienle pipe-resonance system, combining the authenticity of pipe organ sound with the flexibility of digital organ technology.

'My organ is my orchestra.'

A well-known saying by the French organist and composer Cesar Franck, the phrase describes the richness of tone and versatility inherent in the organ. The Concerto Jubileo 40 offers a wide palette of foundation stops, compound stops, and reed stops. Well-known solo stops such as Cornet, Vox Humana, Trompet, or Sesquialter are all present, enabling musicians to explore the vast tonal colors of the ancient pipe organ tradition.

Kienle® Pipe-Resonator System

Based on the ancient pipe organ

A pipe organ produces sound using numerous pipes, each imitating various instruments — trumpets, flutes, oboes — or unique organ voices such as principals and octaves. Stops select which pipes are engaged when keys are played. The pipe’s size, shape, and material influence the sound. Principals produce resonant tones, flutes softer ones, mixtures create bright harmonics, and reeds like the 16’ trumpet produce low, dark tones.

What are Kienle® systems?

The German organ builder Ewald Kienle (1928–2022) pioneered the combination of digital organ sound production with genuine pipe resonance by embedding speakers inside organ pipes. This patented technology precisely controls sound development within pipes, delivering authentically rich pipe organ sound and resonance even in varying temperature conditions. Content Organs is the exclusive global supplier of Kienle® pipe-resonator systems.
