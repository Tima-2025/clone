Cantate 354

With Content Organs' Cantate series, you experience the maximum capabilities of Content Invention Technology®. The Cantate series does not compromise on quality.

An impressive organ

In both technology and sound reproduction, Content makes no compromises with the models from the Cantate series. With as many as 12 independent audio channels, each equipped with dynamic 180W amplifiers, optimal sound detailing has been achieved. The samples, of high quality, are carefully chosen and voiced for the internal audio system. By implementing 4 independent surround channels radiating to the sides and top of the organ case, the experience of the digital organ compared to the pipe organ is closer than ever.

Cabinet and finishing

The Cantate 354R is characterized by an impressive cabinet with illuminated drawknobs. Optionally, this model also offers mechanically movable drawknobs. The solid oakwood cabinet exudes a robust and sturdy appearance. LED pedal lighting, a bench with sheet music storage, and wooden swell pedals—including General Crescendo—are, of course, always included.

The Content Cantate Series is available with a 30- or 32-note flat or (radial) concave pedalboard. Adding toe pistons or a large setzer (Divisional Setzer System) to this organ is, of course, possible. The 128 ExtraVoice voices with touch sensitivity complete this organ.

A wide palette of tonal colors

"My organ is my orchestra" is a well-known saying of the French organist and composer Cesar Franck. More precisely, the art of registration cannot be summarized. An organ has principal voices (principals and flutes), chorus voices (mixture, sharp, etc.), and reeds (trumpet, vox humana, trombone). Various voices can also be used individually as solo stops.

Well-known solo stops on Dutch organs include the Cornet, Vox Humana, Trumpet, or Sesquialter. On the Content Cantate 354 organs, these stops can be found. Discover the richness of possibilities and tonal colors of the ancient pipe organ with the Cantate 354!
