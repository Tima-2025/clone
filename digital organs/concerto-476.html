Concerto 476

Everything comes together in the Content Concerto 476. The grandeur of a true concert organ, combined with the highest achievable sound experience, makes the Concerto 476 a pinnacle. With no less than four unique tonal worlds and a complete palette of stops, every organ piece sounds like a dream. Additionally, over 50 historical tunings are available, and this organ boasts 12 Convolution Reverb reverberations. An audio system with 12 channels and built-in surround sound provides optimal dispersion in the space, thanks to the more than 20 built-in speakers.

Impressive Cabinet with Drawknobs

The Concerto 476R is characterized by an impressive cabinet with drawknobs. Optionally, this model also offers mechanically movable drawknobs. The cabinet, made of solid oak, has a robust and durable appearance. LED pedal lighting, a bench with music storage, and wooden expression pedals – including the General Crescendo – are, of course, always included. The Content Concertos are available with 30 or 32-note flat or (radiating) concave pedals. Adding toe pistons or a large setter (Divisional Setter System) to this organ is, of course, no problem. The 128 ExtraVoice stops with touch sensitivity complete this organ.

Suitable for All Organ Music - Four Organ Styles

The organ features four organ styles, allowing for the performance of all organ literature. Music by composers such as Bach is authentically reproduced with samples from the North German Baroque style. With just one touch, you can switch to the French Romantic style, suitable for works by composers like Widor, Guilmant, or Franck. Naturally, a Dutch organ is included. This disposition is crafted for the rendition of music spanning the breadth of Dutch chorale art.

"My Organ is My Orchestra" - Selecting Stops

'My organ is my orchestra' is a well-known saying by the French organist and composer Cesar Franck. More fittingly, the art of registration cannot be summarized. An organ consists of foundation stops (prestants and flutes), compound stops (Mixtuur, Scherp, etc.), and reed stops (Trompet, Vox Humana, Bazuin). Various stops can also be used individually as solo stops. Well-known solo stops on Dutch organs include the Cornet, Vox Humana, Trompet, or Sesquialter. On the Content Concerto, you can find these stops. Discover the richness of possibilities and tonal colors of the ancient pipe organ!
