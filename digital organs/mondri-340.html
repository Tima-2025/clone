Mondri 340

The Content Mondri series has been a well-established and consistently appreciated choice among organists for many years. The Mondri 340 is the three-manual variant offering 40 stops with four unique organ styles — Baroque, Romantic, Classical, and Symphonic — built into a sturdy positive oak cabinet. Equipped with wooden drawknobs with LED indication, a multi-channel 3D surround sound system, and a Personal Control Center for customization, it provides the immersive experience of a pipe organ in your living room or small concert hall.

Key Features

  • 3 manuals with touch-sensitive keyboards featuring metal cores
  • 40 stops with four unique tonal specifications: Baroque, Romantic, Classic, Symphonic
  • Built into a solid oak positive organ cabinet, customizable with cornices, moldings, and French-style organ bench
  • Multi-channel built-in 3D surround sound system with speakers located at the bottom, front, and top for realistic sound projection
  • Personal Control Center for easy adjustment of stops, sound styles, volume, reverb, tremulant, and registration presets
  • LED lighting for pedal and music stand, wooden drawknobs with LED indicators, and wooden swell pedals
  • 30-note straight, concave, or radial concave pedalboard options
  • Bench with integrated sheet music storage and additional storage behind the music stand
  • Optional ExtraVoice expansion offers orchestral voices like pan flute, trumpet, piano, and more

Sound Experience

The Mondri 340 offers an authentic pipe organ experience with spatial sound dynamics. The organist can select different virtual listening positions in the virtual church space with a single button press, altering acoustic conditions dynamically. The sound positioning emphasizes differences between divisions, with the Swell division sounding more distant compared to the Positive located behind the organist. This provides dynamic depth and realism.

Tonal Color Palette

The organ's tonal color palette includes principal voices such as prestants and flutes, foundation stops like mixtures and sharps, and reeds including trumpet, vox humana, and trombone. Solo registers like Cornet, Vox Humana, and Sesquialter allow expressive registration. Many traditional stops typical of Dutch organs feature prominently.
