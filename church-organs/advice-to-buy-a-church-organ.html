Advice to Buy a Church Organ

The decision to purchase a (new) church organ for your congregation involves many considerations:

  • Is a pipe organ the right instrument, or a digital organ?
  • What space is available for an organ in your church hall?
  • What role will the organ play in the liturgy in the future?
  • How many stops (registers) does the organ need?

We provide advice here to help make this important decision easier. For personalized guidance, please contact Content Organs or visit our factory in Ermelo.

1. Which organ is suitable for your church?

When thinking of a church organ, many imagine a large pipe organ seen in historical churches noted for rich organ history. While magnificent, these may not be feasible for many modern churches due to space, budget, and function.

Content Organs can recommend a suitable organ for every church space, ranging from small portative instruments to larger multi-manual organs. The size directly impacts affordability and how well the organ fulfills its liturgical function.

For digital church organs, choice and tuning are simpler: digital instruments can be fine-tuned via their own intonation software to suit any church space regardless of size. Budget differences between larger and smaller digital organs are minimal. The main focus is the organ's function—supporting congregational singing or fulfilling other liturgical roles.

With years of experience, Content builds and intonates digital organs tailor-made for any church or hall. Standard audio solutions are complemented by options such as true digital pipe organs (resonator systems) and hybrid organs.

2. A digital pipe organ?

Content Organs focuses on craftsmanship and sound quality to approach the ideal of a pipe organ in digital form. Partnering with Kienle® Pipe Resonator Systems, Content offers true digital pipe organs.

Each pipe facade pipe houses a speaker, capable of reproducing digital organ sounds which resonate in the pipe creating rich, authentic pipe-like sound. This patented technology precisely controls sound development in pipes under varying conditions, offering a true digital pipe organ experience.

These systems have proven successful in many churches and are highly regarded by organists.

3. A Hybrid organ?

A hybrid organ features both speaking pipes and digital speakers sharing the tonal palette. Content Organs partners with the Italian organ builder Consoli to create hybrid systems.

  • Foundation of the organ (e.g., Principals 8’, 4’, 2’) uses real pipes
  • Additional voices come digitally through speakers
  • Integration through software and temperature sensors

This solution can be an entirely new hybrid installation or an upgrade to existing pipe organs with digital additions, offering versatility and broad tonal range.

4. Which organ and how many stops?

Choice depends on the organ's liturgical function and preferences of the organists. Generally, a two-manual instrument with 30-40 stops suffices for most congregations.

Content Organs digital organs provide four distinct organ styles (Baroque, Classical, Symphonic, Romantic), enabling a rich combination of preferences and musical genres.

The number of stops should correspond with church size: a small chapel needs fewer stops than a large church seating up to 1000. Content Organs collaborates with you and your organist team, sometimes after an on-site visit, to determine the best disposition and composition.

Thanks to flexible software-based design, digital church organs offer great adaptability.

5. Appearance and finish of a digital church organ

Sound quality is essential, but the organ should also be visually appealing. Important factors include:

  • Quality and finish of organ casing: Content uses oak wood and maintains high craftsmanship standards ensuring durability and beauty.
  • Speaker system finishing: Speaker enclosures use wood with damping materials to prevent resonance, designed for reliability and optimal sound dispersion.
  • Reuse existing material or new casing: Existing pipe facades can be preserved while integrating new audio systems. Alternatively, new or even movable organ casings can be designed to your preferences.

6. Visit with your church or organ committee!

The most important advice is to experience playing and hearing the organs firsthand. We encourage visits to our Ermelo factory or dealers to play and listen to Content Organs.
