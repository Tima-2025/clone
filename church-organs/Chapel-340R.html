Chapel 340R

The Content Chapel 340R is a model that comes standard with a 6-channel external audio system, with internal speakers optionally added, of course. The use of modular speaker systems allows the radiation and position of the speakers to be determined and changed on-site. Connecting additional (bass) speakers is also a possibility. Thanks to the proprietary Content Contenation® software, voicing to the space and location has never been so straightforward.

Four total different organ sounds

The four different tonal worlds (each with 40 speaking voices) are composed of voices from various historical pipe organs in Europe. The sound is characterized by clarity and carrying power, making the organ highly suitable for accompanying congregational singing.

When using the Classic style (à la Bätz-Witte), you can accompany your congregation with powerful principals, warm flutes, and beautiful accompanying stops like the Cornet and Sesquialter. As you build further, you'll get a magnificent plenum, complemented by reeds and mixture stops.

Illuminated drawknobs

The Chapel 340R comes standard in a model with illuminated drawknobs on both sides of the manuals. This provides the model with the optimal experience of a pipe organ. The organ case is, of course, standard with a roll-top cover. Attractive options, such as an LED-lit music stand or wooden-filled keyboards, complete the overall organ experience.

Cabinet

The Chapel 340R is built in an oak cabinet, with various color options available. Content's cabinetmakers work daily on selecting, processing, and coloring pre-selected oak wood. As a result, the Content Chapel 340R is a delight for the ears and eyes in your church or concert hall.

Intonation on every detail!... Contentation

An important element in Content's church organs is the ability to intonate an organ entirely to one's taste and with an eye for the specific characteristics of the (church) space. With a software program developed specifically for this purpose (Contenation®), it is possible to build the disposition and settings of the organ entirely from scratch.

This includes, among other things, the ability to distribute stops across various outputs, adjust the tonal character, volumes, and tremulants per key and per stop, or fine-tune the voicing to the acoustics of the space. Detail-oriented finishing!

General specifications

  • 3 velocity sensitive manuals
  • 4x 40 lighted drawknobs
  • 4 unique organ styles
  • External speakers (monitor system optional)
  • Suitable for churches with approximately 800 people (depending on church size)
