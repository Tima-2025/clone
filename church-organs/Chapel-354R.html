Chapel 354R

The Chapel 354R brings together everything optimally. This model in a solid oak organ console is delivered as standard with wooden drawknobs on either side of the manuals. This provides the model with the optimal experience of a pipe organ. Various pedal, bench, and manual variants complete the comfort for every organist. Additionally, this organ features the advanced Content Chapel audio system, with built-in monitor speakers as options for larger spaces.

Various cultures in one organ

The four different tonal worlds included as standard with this organ have been compiled by Content from voicing material sourced from various historical pipe organs in Europe. The tonal character is characterized by clarity and carrying capacity. The German Baroque style is a representative representation in the style of organ builders such as Schnitger, the Dutch Classical organ is influenced by builders Bätz and Witte, the French Romantic tonal world is modeled after Cavaillé-Coll organs, and for the English Symphonic world, various organs from the English choral tradition served as inspiration.

For every church building

With the aforementioned advanced audio system, various speaker configurations are possible. This makes the Chapel 354R one of the most flexible church organs from Content. There is the option to give each division (manual) its own physical position. This can, for example, symbolize a historical organ façade, a choir arrangement as customary in English cathedrals, or a unique setup fitting within your (church) space. There is also the possibility to position various stops on separate channels or speakers, such as placing soft strings from the Swell division at a position above the organ façade. Thus, in collaboration with organists and church members, a custom organ façade is realized.

High-quality organ sound through multi-channel audio systems

"Quality is our most important stop." This promise is one that Content Organs is proud to uphold. An included 8-channel audio system can be expanded to as many as 32 separate audio channels, allowing for the highest quality and expansive tonal representation. Since each channel has various speakers, it is possible to fulfill the desire for "each speaker having a stop." This significantly enhances the carrying capacity of the instrument, especially when it comes to accompanying congregational or choral singing.
