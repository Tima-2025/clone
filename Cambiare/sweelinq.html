Playing world-famous pipe organs yourself...

Sweelinq (a product of Orgelmakerij Noorlander) is a virtual organ sampler software package. They, just like Hauptwerk, make recordings of world-famous pipe organs. The biggest difference is the payment method, as Sweelinq uses a subscription model. Hauptwerk offers individual sample sets for which you pay a fixed price per sample set.

After the recordings are made, they are combined to create a sample set. A sample set is a collection of recordings (samples) of a specific organ. This sample set has an extension that allows these sounds to be played through the Sweelinq software package. The aforementioned recording process requires a significant amount of time and effort, but thanks to this investment, organists worldwide can play famous and lesser-known pipe organs in their own living rooms.

A Sweelinq sample set consists of a combination of numerous recordings. Per pipe, per register (combination), and at various locations within the space. When you activate a register and press a key, many actions are sent simultaneously. This naturally increases when you play with multiple registers or in polyphony. Only a powerful computer can handle this 'communication' smoothly and without interruptions. Various differences and similarities

Digital Organ or Sweelinq Organ?

First of all, at Content, you can find both types of organs!

With our extensive experience (since 1980), we possess in-depth knowledge of pipe organs, audio systems, amplification, and optimal sound reproduction. Both solutions are of high quality.

One significant difference lies in the way the sound is generated. With a Sweelinq organ, you play pre-recorded samples made in the respective churches. These recordings have been captured at various locations within the church in more recent sample sets.

It’s also possible to customize the playback position yourself. This way, you’ll hear the organ exactly as it sounds in the chosen church space. This brings a famous church to life in your living room. Listen to our samples for an impression!

In the case of a digital organ, pre-recorded samples from various churches are combined to create four different organ styles (North German Baroque, Dutch Romantic, French Romantic, and English Symphonic soundscapes). Each organ style has its sample library with so-called 'clean source samples' from which a selection is made when creating the dispositions of a digital organ.

These 'clean samples' are recordings that faithfully reproduce the pipe organ sound as purely as possible, without spatial effects. Together, an ensemble of samples is created, representing the respective organ style. As an organist, you have control over how these samples are heard in the church space. For example, you can determine the position within the church, add or remove reverb, adjust tunings, add tremolos, and apply various other digital adjustments to the existing source sample.

This way, you play an organ that is entirely customized and fine-tuned according to your preferences in your living room!
